GIS Analysis

Carbon Analysis at Watersheed Area - FOLU Net Sink 2030

A Q1-level geospatial and carbon analysis project was conducted to investigate Soil Organic Carbon (SOC) loss throughout the Sepaku Watershed. The study utilized Sentinel-2 NDVI, DEMNAS-derived topography, SWAT+ hydrological modeling, and erosion simulations to develop key spatial layers, including the LULC map, erosion susceptibility map, and elevation model. The analysis assessed how erosion influences carbon redistribution, quantified carbon loss, calculated the Carbon Enrichment Ratio (CER), and identified areas most vulnerable to SOC depletion.

Erosion Modeling (SWAT+) Raster Processing Sentinel Imagery Carbon Accounting
Machine Learning Engineering

Predictive Model for Forest Fire in East Kalimantan

Developed a Random Forest based predictive model to identify forest fire risk in East Kalimantan using MODIS hotspot data from 2014 to 2023 and seven environmental parameters from ERA5 Land. Threshold analysis based on FWI classes revealed clear environmental shifts toward fire-prone conditions, including precipitation dropping from 0.71 mm/day to 0.002 mm/day, and surface temperature increasing to 29.29°C. Three modeling scenarios were evaluated with the best scenario reaching an accuracy of 99.15%.

Python (Pandas, NumPy) Machine Learning Geospatial Analysis

LDA Topic Modeling for Forest Fire Research

Conducted a comprehensive bibliometric and topic modeling analysis using the Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) method to investigate research trends on forest and land fires in Indonesia. The study analyzed 1,312 publications from 2000 to 2024 and identified five dominant themes. Findings reveal that Kalimantan and Sumatra are the most frequently studied regions, closely linked with meteorological drivers such as temperature, rainfall, and major climate phenomena like El Niño.
